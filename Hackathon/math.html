Only twenty-eight percent of positions in the mathematics profession are held by women. Let's change that

Challenges faced by Women in Math:

  • Societal stereotypes and bias
  • Underrepresentation
  • Unequal oppurtunities
  • Unfair wages
  • Work-life balance

Although the number of women in math is steadily increasing, it will take at least 30 years until there are equal numbers of men and women in this proffession

The mathematics community grapples with a pervasive gender gap, leading to the underrepresentation of women in tenured faculty positions. Additonally, women are less likely to recieve prestigious awards and leadership roles in the field. Women mathematicians often contend with systemic barriers, including implicit bias, stereotypes, and unequal oppurtunities for advancement and recognition Efforts to address these challenges and promote gender equality in mathematics involve initiatives aimed at increasing access to quality education, providing mentorship and support networks, challenging stereotypes, and advocating for policy changes within academia and beyond. Achieving true gender equality in mathematics requires sustained dedication and collaboration from individuals, organizations, and policymakers to create inclusive and equitable environments where women mathematicians can thrive and contribute fully to the field."

Overcoming these barriers is no easy task...

However, there are many women that have overcome these challenges and risen to greatness!

Let's acknowledge incredible female mathematicians and their acheivements

  • Edith Clarke: Invented the graphical calculator that greatly simplified the calculations necessary to determine characteristics of long electrical transmissions lines.
  • Hertha Ayrton: Developed a new type of mathematical line divider that cound split a line precisely into any number of equal parts.
  • Annie Easley: Worked on NASA's Centaur technology--a high-energy rocket technology that uses liquid hydrogen and liquid oxygen to boost rockets into space.
  • Grace Hopper
